Weiss Stories


Jacob Moses Weiss (1768 - 1833) and Gella Bloch Weiss (1770 - ??) were born, lived and died in Floss. Jacob supposedly received "protection" (Schutz) in 1793 which permitted him to legally reside, marry and have children. Jacob and Gella had, according to what we know, two sons and three daughters.

Their eldest child Bella (Betti) Weiss Leinkauf (1796 - 1852), from what we can determine, married a Herman Leinkauf and lived in Pressburg (today known as Bratislava, the capitol of Slovakia). Here are details concerning that family.

Their next child was Isaak Loeb Weiss (1800 - 1884). He married Semele (Sophia) Hoenigsberger (1811 - 1872). They were both from Floss, and lived there from birth to death. They had ten children. Here are details concerning that family.

There next child was a daughter Feigle (Fradel, Francziska) Weiss Prager (abt 1805 - 1871) who, like her sister Bella, went to Pressburg where she married Mordechai (Markus) Prager (abt 1799 - 1877). See here for a page (in Hebrew) from the Pinkas HaMohelim, where they are mentioned. They had 10 children. This family had many rabbis and other religious leaders in central europe. Huge numbers thereof were murdered in the Holocaust. Many of their descendents can be found in Bnei Barak, Jerusalem, Brooklyn and Monsey. Details are on my Ancestry page.

Then there was their daughter Babette (Breindl) Weiss Boscowitz (1810 - 1890). This is my great-great-grandmother. (Hopefully this will help you figure out your relationship to these people.) She had eight children. Here are details concerning that family.

Their youngest child was a son Abraham Weiss (1814 - 1888). He emigrated to Mobile, Alabama. He married, had a child, was widowed, remarried and had a further eightt children. Here are details concerning his family.
