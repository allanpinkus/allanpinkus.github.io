Sabine


Sabine Boscowitz was born on January 28, 1921 in Weiden in der Oberpfalz (northeast Bavaria) to Johanna Oppenheimer and Albert Boscowitz. Here is her birth certificate:



Click on the documents and photo to see them in more detail.

Sabine left Germany for England on April 1, 1939. Sabine and her friend Bertl Baum were able to get out of Germany with the help of Kossy and Bertha Strauss who had moved from Weiden to Birmingham. Kossy was the son of their Jewish school teacher Emanuel Strauss, while Bertha was the elder sister of their good friend Hedwig Sterzelbach (whose parents were best friends of Sabine's parents). Sabine first worked as a "domestic servant" for the Buxton family who lived in Burton-upon-Trent. Her "letter of recommendation" from Erica Buxton, of which she was rather proud, was the following: Sabine Boscowitz was here as a Domestic help from April 1939 to February 1940. She is very cheerful and willing and I found her perfectly honest. She is an intelligent girl, and I formed the impression that she would probably do well in any job in which she was interested. She was very good with young children and helped in the management of the baby from the first month. Unfortunately she was not interested in general domestic work, as she had her heart set on Nursing as a profession. In these circumstances I could hardly judge of her capabilities. Here is a listing from the 1939 Register of England & Wales. And this "Female Enemy Alien - Exemption from Internment Refugee"

Note that in both these forms Sabine is listed with a middle initial "S" or a middle name "Sara". She had no middle initial or name. The Nazis gave all Jewish women the middle name of Sara.

Despite the above Sabine was subsequently interned on the Isle of Man.
