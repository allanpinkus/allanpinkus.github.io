Guttmann Family


    1. Introduction:

    This is a story about some of the Guttmann family. We start with Isaac Guttmann and Katherine Leinkauf Guttmann. But then mainly follow the line of Hermann Eisner Guttmann and Alfred Eisner Guttmann. We tell a bit about the firm Guttmann and Frank. And then add some comments and references with regards to the other parts of the family. Many details of the story remain obscure. As with most family histories reconstructed from surviving documents, the record is incomplete and often raises as many questions as it answers. But we were surprised by how much we did uncover and discover. Please feel free to contact us if you can add to or correct anything herein.

    2. Isaac and Katherine:

    Isaac Guttmann was born in Oct 1840 in the town of Reca which is near Senec. Senec is about 30 km east of Pressburg. Pressburg was the German name for the city of Bratislava, Slovakia. This entire area was at the time in the Hungarian part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. The Jews of this area spoke German, and perhaps also Hungarian. Isaac was the son of Moses Guttmann (abt 1790 – 1874 Rete, Slovakia) and Miriam Regner Guttmann (1810 – Feb 10, 1900 Rete, Slovakia). Isaac first married Regina Blau (1843 – 1887) on 8 Aug 1862 in Dunaszerdahely (about 50 km southeast of Pressburg). Regina and Isaac had two daughters. Julie was born on 25 Aug 1866 and Hermine (Hani) was born on 26 Nov 1869. Both were probably born in Dunaszerdahely. Isaac and Regina's families seem to have been traditional Orthodox Jews. The family story was that Regina died young, leading Isaac to remarry. While it is true that Regina died while fairly young, that is aged 44, (see here) the fact is that Isaac and Regina divorced. This is noted both on Isaac’s second marriage record (see here) and on Regina’s death record. It was a family secret. We can only speculate as to what happened. Perhaps she had a mental breakdown or terrible postnatal depression. We can only speculate.

    Katherine (Katalin) Leinkauf was born in 1836 in Pressburg. Katherine was, from we can determine, one of six children of Hermann Leinkauf and Bella Weiss (born about 1796 in Floss, Germany – 9 Dec 1852, Pressburg). Four of Katherine's siblings emigrated from Pressburg to Mobile, Alabama, over some years in the mid 19th century. They had an uncle Abraham Weiss who had previously emigrated from Floss to Mobile. Katherine and a sister named Minna remained in Pressburg. On 12 June 1864 Katherine married Max Eisner in Ganserndof, which is also near Pressburg, (see here). Max Eisner was born about 1828 in Pressburg. We have no details as to when he died, but it was before 1874. Katherine and Max had two sons, namely Hermann, born 21 Mar 1865 in Vienna, and Alfred, born 19 Feb 1866, also in Vienna.

    In May of 1874, in Pressburg, the widow Katherine Leinkauf Eisner married the divorced Isaac Guttmann, (see here). The marriage was conducted at the Neolog (or conservative) synagogue in Pressburg. Marrying in a Neolog synagogue seems a significant break from Orthodox tradition. But this was becoming more and more common. Isaac officially adopted Katherine's two sons, and thus their family name became Guttmann. Katherine and Isaac then had two additional daughters, namely Berta born on 7 Apr 1875 in Pressburg, and Malvina born on 7 Oct 1876 also in Pressburg. Katherine and Isaac raised all 6 children, i.e., Katherine's two boys with Max Eisner, Isaac's two daughters with Regina Blau, and their two joint daughters.

    Isaac and Katalin moved to Novi Sad, in the province of Vojvodina in what is today Serbia where Isaac acquired large land holdings. They owned a large working quarry at Krcedin, about 40km from Novi Sad, where the land included fields, meadows, vineyards and buildings. More about this in Section ??. By the time Isaac died in 1906, they were living in Váci Street, a prime location in the middle of Budapest, close to the Danube river. Isaac headed the firm I. Guttmann and Sons. What exactly this was is unclear.

    Isaac died in Budapest on 4 Jan 1908 at the age of 65, (see here). An announcement of his death appeared in Pester Lloyd a German-language online daily newspaper from Budapest, (see here and here). Katherine died of a stroke on 22 Mar 1915 in Budapest at the age of 79, (see here). Katherine and Isaac are buried in the Kozma Street Jewish cemetery in Budapest.     2. Hermann and his children:

    As was stated above, Hermann was born Hermann Eisner on 21 Mar 1865 in Vienna. He and his brother Alfred were adopted by Isaac Guttmann and took the family name of Guttmann. Here and here are the adoption papers. We could not decipher a date on these papers. We assume it was about 1874. In 1895 Hermann married Margit Ungar. Here is their wedding certificate. We were only able to decipher the year. Margit was born on 17 Oct 1876 in Gyékényes, Hungary. She was the daughter of Samuel Ungar and Paula Kremsier Ungar. Hermann's main business seems to have centered in Novi Sad and environs. We will relate more on that later. But he and Margit were often to be found in Budapest. In fact these two cities were only about 300 km from each other. Hermann and Margit had 4 children, all of whom were born in Budapest. They were Georgina (Gyorgyi) born 20 Aug 1899, Luiza (Lutzi) born about 1902, Rudolf (Rudi) born 26 Mar 1905 and Veronia (Vera) born 4 Dec 1910. We have here a copy of Hermann's Yugoslavian citizenship certificate from 1934. His height is listed as 1.68 m, his face is oval in shape, and his hair is grey. In 1939 Hermann and Margit lived at Ulica Petra Zrinskog 2 in Novi Sad (today called Ulica Petra Drapsina). Hermann died on 4 Nov 1939 at the age of 74. The cause of death is listed as arteriosclerosis cardiac paralysis. Hermann is buried in the Novi Sad Jewish Cemetery, (see here). Margit survived the war as, surprisingly, did all four of her children. Margit was for a while in Budapest, in hiding, with her grandson Peter (Rudi's son), his mother Anna, and Anna's parents. After the war Margit returned to Novi Sad and was with Rudi and family. In December 1948 they emigrated to Israel. She then lived with her daughter ??? in Gedera, Israel. She died there in 1964 at the age of 88.

    Georgina (Gyorgi) was born on 20 Aug 1899 in Budapest. She was the eldest daughter of Hermann and Margit. On 30 Apr 1918 she married Dr. Armin (Hermann) Semsei in Budapest. He was born on 30 Mar 1886 in the Pusztaszetlorinc neighbourhood of Budapest. Together they had two sons, Gabriel Stefan Semsei born 25 Jan 1919 in Budapest, and Tamas (Thomas) Otto Semsei born 16 Dec 1922 in Budapest. They lived at 54 Columbus utca in Budapest. We do not know what Armin did. We do not know when and how he died. We do know that Armin and Georgina (sometimes also called Stefania) were members of the Brit HaZohar B'Hungaria, i.e, Zionist revisionist group in Hungary. As was their son Tamas. On later forms to be found in the Arolsen Archives Georgina wrote that from "March 1944 to February 1945 I was hidden in a dark cellar room in Budapest", see here and here. Georgina survived the war, as did her sons. She remained in Budapest until 5 Jan 1950 when she emigrated from Hungary to Israel. She came on the boat "Comijut". In Israel she initially lived in Gedera, probably with one of her sisters. On 8 Aug 1952 she married Dr. Otto Kraus in Tel-Aviv. Dr. Otto Kraus was a chest physician. He was born in Austria on 8 Mar 1893. He was interred in Theresienstadt, survived, and immediately after the war became the official doctor for the American Joint Distribution Committee in Prague, see his obituary. In the meantime it seems that her son Gabriel had emigrated to Sydney, Australia. In March of 1955 Georgina, her husband Otto and her son Tamas also arrived in Sydney. Gabriel had a store, married and had a daughter. Tamas, who was now officially Thomas, became a very successful Tyre distributor. In fact he received an award from the Israel-Australia Chamber of Commerce in 1976, and the "Medal of the Order of Australia" from the Australian government in 1997 "For Service to Business Through Marketing in the Tyre Industry". Otto died on 8 Feb 1972 , Gabriel on 17 Nov 1977, Georgina on 11 Jul 1983 and Thomas on 3 August 2008. They are all buried in the Macquarie Park Cemetery in Sydney.
